Crew Training and Preparation Protocol

1. CREW SELECTION CRITERIA

Physical Requirements:
  • Age Range: 28-55 years
  • Physical Fitness: Advanced level
  • Vision: 20/20 (correction allowed)
  • Blood Pressure: <140/90
  • No chronic medical conditions
Educational Requirements:
  • Master's degree or higher in:
    • Engineering
    • Physical Sciences
    • Biological Sciences
    • Mathematics
  • Minimum 3 years professional experience
  • Flight experience preferred

2. TRAINING PROGRAM (24 MONTHS)

Phase 1: Basic Training (6 months)

  • Spacecraft systems familiarization
  • EVA (spacewalk) basics
  • Physical conditioning
  • Survival training
  • Basic medical procedures
  • Communication protocols

Phase 2: Advanced Training (8 months)

  • Detailed spacecraft operations
  • Advanced EVA training
  • Lunar landing procedures
  • Scientific equipment operation
  • Emergency response scenarios
  • Team dynamics and leadership

Phase 3: Mission Specific Training (10 months)

  • Mission simulation exercises
  • Lunar surface operations
  • Specific experiment procedures
  • Full mission rehearsals
  • Launch and re-entry procedures

3. PHYSICAL CONDITIONING

Daily Requirements:
  • 2 hours physical exercise
  • Cardiovascular training
  • Resistance training
  • Balance and coordination
  • Spatial orientation exercises
Special Training:
  • Neutral buoyancy training
  • Centrifuge training
  • Zero-gravity flight experience
  • Pressure suit conditioning
  • Long-duration isolation practice

4. TECHNICAL TRAINING

Spacecraft Systems:
  • Navigation systems
  • Life support systems
  • Communication equipment
  • Power systems
  • Propulsion systems
  • Emergency systems
Scientific Operations:
  • Experiment procedures
  • Sample collection
  • Equipment maintenance
  • Data collection and analysis
  • Photography and documentation

5. PSYCHOLOGICAL PREPARATION

Individual Training:
  • Stress management
  • Sleep regulation
  • Decision making under pressure
  • Isolation coping strategies
  • Risk assessment
Team Training:
  • Crew coordination
  • Conflict resolution
  • Cross-cultural communication
  • Leadership and followership
  • Emergency response coordination

6. MEDICAL TRAINING

Basic Medical Skills:
  • CPR and first aid
  • Injury assessment
  • Basic surgical procedures
  • Medication administration
  • Mental health monitoring
Emergency Procedures:
  • Trauma response
  • Space-specific medical issues
  • Radiation exposure treatment
  • Decompression procedures
  • Medical equipment operation

7. SIMULATION TRAINING

Full Mission Simulations:
  • Launch procedures
  • Orbital operations
  • Lunar landing
  • Surface operations
  • Emergency scenarios
  • Return procedures
Facility Training:
  • Mission Control interaction
  • Communication protocols
  • Problem-solving scenarios
  • Equipment failure response
  • Team coordination exercises

8. FINAL PREPARATION (Last 3 Months)

  • Mission-specific rehearsals
  • Final medical examinations
  • Psychology evaluations
  • Team cohesion assessment
  • Equipment familiarization
  • Mission plan review
  • Family preparation
  • Media training

9. CONTINGENCY TRAINING

Emergency Scenarios:
  • Launch abort procedures
  • Space evacuation
  • Fire response
  • Depressurization
  • Medical emergencies
  • Communication failure
  • Power system failure
  • Navigation system failure

10. EVALUATION CRITERIA

Regular Assessments:
  • Physical fitness tests
  • Technical knowledge exams
  • Psychological evaluations
  • Team performance reviews
  • Emergency response drills
  • Mission simulation performance
  • Medical readiness checks
