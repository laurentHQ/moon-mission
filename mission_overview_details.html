Mission Overview

1. MISSION OBJECTIVES

  • Establish sustainable human presence on the Moon
  • Conduct scientific research and exploration
  • Test new technologies for future Mars missions
  • Utilize lunar resources
  • International collaboration

2. MISSION PHASES

Phase 1: Planning and Preparation (18-24 months)

  • Vehicle and equipment development
  • Crew selection and training
  • Launch site preparation
  • Mission control setup

Phase 2: Launch and Transit (3-4 days)

  • Launch sequence
  • Earth orbit operations
  • Trans-lunar injection
  • Course corrections

Phase 3: Lunar Operations (Initial 30 days)

  • Lunar orbit insertion
  • Landing site approach
  • Surface operations
  • Scientific experiments

Phase 4: Return Journey (3-4 days)

  • Lunar ascent
  • Earth return trajectory
  • Re-entry procedures
  • Recovery operations

3. KEY COMPONENTS

  • Launch vehicle
  • Crew module
  • Lunar lander
  • Life support systems
  • Scientific equipment
  • Communication systems

4. CREW REQUIREMENTS

  • Commander
  • Pilot
  • Mission Specialist
  • Science Officer
  • Medical Officer

5. SAFETY PROTOCOLS

  • Emergency procedures
  • Backup systems
  • Abort scenarios
  • Medical contingencies
  • Equipment redundancy

6. MISSION TIMELINE

T-24 months: Begin final preparation phase
T-12 months: Complete vehicle assembly
T-6 months: Final crew training
T-3 months: Launch site preparation
T-1 month: Final checks and reviews
T-0: Launch
T+3 days: Lunar orbit insertion
T+4 days: Lunar landing
T+34 days: Mission completion
T+37 days: Earth return
